Don't Panic !

Using Wikimedia by moulin does not require any particular skill: only a little bit of practice.

You can think of the content of this distribution like a paper encyclopedia whose pages would not be sorted.

How do I access the content ?

While you can't browse pages one after another to reach the good one, moulin provides much more powerful tools: the search engine and the links.

The links are colored (blue) and underlined text areas. Pointing the mouse hover makes the cursor transform into a «hand». This is a sign indicating that this text is associated with some action. Left-clicking on the link makes the current page disapear to show a new one, announced by the link.

Wikimedia projects are heavily based on links because of their ability to link articles with others. For example, an article on the french language may propose links to the articles about alphabet, latin, or even France ; this is the richness of this tool: depending on the author's point of view, each word can link to its definition (or it's article) or a related subject.

The search engine acts as a links generator. You can find it on the toolbar, located on the top of the window. You can enter one or more words in it called «keywords» ; then you valid by pressing enter and a new page appears. This new page present a list of links to articles whose names contains your keywords.

For example, if you want to know the name of the president of France, you can enter searchs like «president» or «france» or even «president france». Those keywords should be choosed carefully because if none is contained in an article title, you won't get any result : you would then have to retry another search with less-specific keywords.

What can I do with moulin ?

moulin is a resource holder. It gives you access to the various content of the Wikimedia projects.

Here's a list of things you can do with it:

  • Search who is the president, what is the population or the spoken language of any country in the world
  • Read biographies of numerous important people
  • Discover new species of Plants and Animals
  • Learn the history of famous ancient civilizations

The power of this tool is links. Since all pages are cross-linked, you can be rambling in the Wikipedia, starting from any search, just following links as you are interested in specific subjects.

If you don't know where to start, just click a link in the homepage.

How do I launch moulin ?

Launching on Windows

  • moulin is installed:
    1. Double Click on Start -> Programs -> moulin -> moulin.
  • moulin is not installed
    1. Insert the media (CD-ROM, USB drive).
    2. When the autorun menu appears, click on `Launch moulin now` ; done.
    3. If nothing appear, launch the Windows Explorer (Computer icon on the desktop).
    4. Go inside the media drive.
    5. Double-click on `moulin.exe` inside the `moulin` folder.

Launching on Linux

  1. Insert the media (CD-ROM, USB drive).
  2. Go to moulin's mounted volume
  3. Launch `moulin-linux`

You can do it either from any file manager or from the console:

$ /media/moulin/moulin/moulin-linux

Launching on Mac OS X

  1. Insert the media (CD-ROM, USB drive).
  2. In the Finder, go to the `moulin` folder of the disk.
  3. Double-click on the windmill icon: `moulin-app`

How do I use moulin ?

Once you have launched moulin, your screen should look like:

Welcome Screen

As you can see, there are 2 main areas in the moulin software: the toolbar (on top) and the content area.

The toolbar

The toolbar aims to help you with global navigation. It's scope is the current page (the content area).

Detailed toolbar

Explainations:

  • home: Return to the homepage (the one which welcomed you)
  • back: Return to the previous page you visited. Probably the one from which you clicked a link.
  • forward: While navigating through your browsing history (using the back button), moves you forward on that list of visited pages.
  • search input & search button: Insert any text in the search field, hit the search button and a search results page will be displayed on screen
  • print: Prints the current page on your printer.
  • text bigger: Makes all texts on the current page appear bigger.
  • text smaller: Makes all texts on the current page appear smaller.
  • search in page: Helps you search for a particular word or sentence in the current page.
  • fullscreen: Makes the software use your entire screen size for better reading.
  • side bar: Toggle the side bar which provides Bookmarks and Notes features.

The content area

Wikipedia articles are all linked, or cross-referenced. Wherever you see highlighted text like this, it means there is a link to some relevant article or Wikipedia page with further in-depth information elsewhere if you need it. You are always one click away from more information on any point that has a link attached. There are other links towards the ends of most articles, for other articles of interest, relevant external web sites and pages, reference material, and organized categories of knowledge which you can search and traverse in a loose hierarchy for more information.

Can I install moulin on my computer ?

Installing moulin on your hard drive grants you huge speed improvments because your hard drive is much faster than you CD-Drive. If your are using moulin out of a usb flash-drive, speed difference is reduced but your usb-stick life-time would increase.

Installing on Windows

  1. Insert your media (CD-ROM, flash-drive) into the computer
  2. If nothing appends:
    1. Go to the Windows Explorer (the `Computer` icon on your desktop)
    2. Right-click on the moulin's drive and select `Explore`.
    3. Go to the `autorun` folder.
    4. Double-click on the moulin-autorun.exe icon.
  3. moulin launcher is on the screen, click on the `Install moulin to your HDD` button and follow instructions

Installing on Linux

  1. Copy the `moulin` folder from the moulin media to a place in your hard-drive

You can do it either from any file manager or from the console:

$ cp -r /media/moulin/moulin ~/

Installing on Mac OS X

  1. From the finder, drag the moulin folder (not just the app contained in it) to any place on your Hard Drive (/Applications is good)

You can then drag the app in the folder you dragged to you Dock.
